Trading / Commerce Guide

How Trade Week works, how Local vs Remote prices behave, and how to use mansion slots and guild techniques to farm Commerce Coins.

1. What Trading Goods are

Each Trade Week, the game picks three Trading Goods: one Local Fluctuation item and two Remote Fluctuation items. You buy them from normal shops on your own world, then sell them in your world or in other players’ worlds for Commerce Coins.

When a new Trade Week starts, the three goods rotate. Old-week goods are still items in your bag but are no longer part of the structured Trading loop for that week.

2. Important resets (UTC)

  • Trade Week reset: Friday 21:00 UTC.
    The three Trading Goods rotate; the Price Bulletin changes and its “Time remaining” timer resets.
  • Weekly reset: Sunday 21:00 UTC.
    Treat this as the point where vendor purchase amounts have refreshed at least once. If you sold early, you can usually buy another batch of this week’s goods.

Hard rule: aim to have no Trading Goods in your bag when Friday 21:00 UTC hits. Dump everything before the next Trade Week.

3. Price Bulletin & where to buy

You can open the Price Bulletin in two ways:

  • Talk to Zang Qiyue at the Trade Commission in Kaifeng and choose “Price Fluctuations”, or
  • Open the Trade Commission UI and press “Check Announcements”.

The Bulletin shows, for the current Trade Week:

  • Which three items are Trading Goods.
  • Which one is Local and which two are Remote.
  • A “Go to purchase” line for each item that tells you where to buy it on your world.
  • A “Time remaining” countdown to the next Friday 21:00 UTC Trade Week reset.

4. Local vs Remote fluctuation

Local Fluctuation (1 item)

  • On your world, buy and sell price both move up and down during the week; at any moment, buy = sell for you.
  • You want to buy when the % is low and sell when it is clearly high for that week.

Remote Fluctuation (2 items)

  • On your world, the price is fixed; buy and sell don’t change.
  • Other players’ worlds have different prices. You use Remote Price / Co-op sale to find worlds with high prices and sell there.
  • Timing is about when you find good buyers, not about your own daily reset.

5. Inventory, mansion & weekly loop

Slots & the mansion

Most players start with 20 bag slots. Renting the big mansion at Feng’s Tradehall (10 000 Commerce Coins/month) adds +60 slots, giving an effective 80-slot Trading inventory. This is the practical minimum for serious Trading.

There are three house options, but for Trading the mansion is the key one; the extra 60 slots make the method feel worth it.

Later, guild techniques can add more Trading slots. Once you have 90+ slots you simply buy 30 of each weekly good.

Buying pattern (80-slot baseline)

  1. After Friday 21:00 UTC, open the Price Bulletin and sort the three goods by base price.
  2. With 80 slots and a 30-per-item cap, buy:
    • 30 of the most expensive item,
    • 30 of the second most expensive item,
    • 20 of the remaining item.
  3. You now have a full bag optimised for profit; percentage gains apply to the base price, so expensive items return more Coins per %.

Weekly loop

  1. Friday – buy your weekly stock after 21:00 UTC.
  2. Across the week – monitor the Local item % and Remote buyers. Hold until the numbers look good.
  3. Mid-week (roughly Tue–Thu) – often good for Local peaks. Sell if the % looks high for this week.
  4. If you sell early, you can usually re-buy at least once before the next Trade Week, especially after Sunday 21:00 UTC when stocks have refreshed.
  5. Before the next Friday 21:00 UTC – dump any leftovers at the best price you can find.

6. Guild techniques: Selling Bonus & Market Tax

Under Techniques → Wealth → Wealth Through Trade, Chen Shusheng offers two key Trading techniques:

  • Selling Bonus – “Exchange Trading Goods with guild members, profit +X%” (Commerce Coins acquisition +X%).
  • Market Tax – “Exchange Trading Goods with guild members, tax rate −Y%” (Trading Goods transaction tax −Y%).

These only work when trading with guild members, including your guild’s Trade NPC. They do not apply to random public players.

Sell priority if you have these:

  1. Guildmates’ worlds or your guild Trade NPC.
  2. Random high-price buyers (Remote Price) only if they beat your guild-boosted value by a clear margin.
  3. Local sale in your own world as a last resort near the end of the week.

7. Timers & checklists in WWM Helper

WWM Helper exposes Trading through:

  • Trade Week Reset timer – Friday 21:00 UTC, when the three goods rotate and you should clear last week’s stock.
  • Trade Price Check timer – mid-week reminder to log in and check Local % plus Remote buyers.
  • Optional daily checklist items: “Trade – buy weekly goods (Fri→Sun)” and “Trade – check prices and offload goods”.
  • An optional weekly checklist item: “Run your weekly Trading / Commerce loop”.

Use timers for the “when” and the checklist for “did I actually do it”.
